Designing the Information Architecture


Initial Information Architecture

This phase involved:

  • 1. Determining the hierarchy between features and functions
  • 2. Identifying missing features
  • 3. Deciding onboarding flow


Next step was to pick each module and design high level flows for that indivdual module.

Designing the high level flows


A high level flow shows the most important states, without going deep into specific interactions or edge cases. A high level flow helps define the specifications and constraints for the next stage of the process.


High level flows were helpful because:

  • 1. They gave us a better understanding of the information flow in the application.
  • 2. They helped us identify what features are missing or redundant.
  • 3. They provided enough information for the developers to start building the backend.
  • 4. They are fast to design. Hence, during the initial phase of the project, when there is a lot of ambiguity, it is easy to modify them.
a. Onboarding - Phase 1

This phase involved designing flows for different first-time and returning users to be able to get into the app seamlessly. Design contains features based on the requirements provided by the Product Manager.


Challenges in this phase:

  • 1. Handle different user profiles.


  • 2. Making the sign up process easy.


  • 3. Making the user stay while the system loads for 10 seconds.


Corresponding solutions:

  • 1. Creating a login for realtors and inviting the clients through email.

  • 2. Letting the users use their Google or Microsoft login process.

  • 3. Letting the users setup their profile while the system loads.
