Motivation:
As a student who has taken tests like GRE and TOEFL, I wanted to help other students with their preparation to score well.

System Concept Statement:
Wordly is an android app for non-native English speakers to improve their English vocabulary. It helps the users memorize most popular words for those exams.


Process:

Problem Definition


To begin with the research, the first step was to clearly define a problem statement.

Brainstorming

As a group of four developers working towards the same goal we started throwing out ideas and discussing issues which we felt were important and needed addressing. We discussed various aspects of these exams and which we thought were most difficult for us as individuals.
We finalized on focussing on Vocabulary Building for the following reasons:

  • 1. It is required in all aspects of the Verbal section of exams.
  • 2. It is the first step in the preparation of the exams.

Brainstorming Notes

Bug List


As a next step, we started with listing the problems which we faced as individuals along with issues which we thought needed attention. Listing out all the bugs large and small followed by reading up on them and making observations in the world helped us realize the problem more deeply.


Bug List

User Research


Through various discussions as a team, I realized how four of us have different approaches towards the same problems. I realized that it would be worthwhile to immerse into students' lives and know what techniques do they use to understand and memorize words.
Drawing upon the assumptions that developed in the problem definition we developed research objectives, defined key users and stakeholders and decided to perform contextual inquiry.


Defining Stakeholders


We came up with the following stakeholders:

  • 1. Students preparing for exams
  • 2. Educational institutes which provide training for the exams

I wanted to dig deeper into students's lives and identify their pain points where we could find an opportunity area to provide a solution. At the same time, getting to know the practices of educational institutes served as a guideline for us to design solutions around those line. However, creating a product which also incorporates students' unique techniques which can benefit everyone was my main aim.


Contextual Inquiry

This phase involved the following:

  • 1. Interviews with students
  • 2. Interviews with personnels from Educational institutes


After creating an interview script, we recruitred 4 students who were going to take either GRE, TOEFL or SAT. We wanted to know about the difficulties they were facing and what were their current techniques.

Identified pain points:

  • 1. Lack of resources
  • 2. Forgetting the words after memorizing their meanings once
  • 3. Difficulty in testing their progress
  • 4. Translating words in Hindi (most widely spoken language in India)
  • 5. Getting stuck on particular words
Their techniques:

  • 1. Using index cards to memorize words
  • 2. Associating words with stories
  • 3. Revising particular words
  • 4. Practising with friends

I also interviewed Dr. Swati Jain of Swati Jain Education Group, Indore. Swati Jain Education Group was established in 1992 to guide students for higher education in India and abroad.

Teaching practices:

  • 1. Booklets for most popular words for exams. Each booklet containing hundreds of words with meanings and sentence examples.
  • 2. Weekly quizzes for students
  • 3. Grouping all the synonyms and antonyms together
  • 4. Hindi examples

Lessons learned


This project taught me the importance of user-centered design. This project was one of the major reasons which moved me to pursue a masters degree in this field.

One of the challenges in this project was to design an easy onboarding process while keeping in mind the development limitations. This required a lot of iterations and working closely with the developers to tackle this.
