Sketch and Photography

My eagerness in solving problems is something that I borrowed from my father, while I inherited creativity from my mother.

Observing my father’s tireless efforts towards resolving grievances of the under-represented sections of my society as an administrative officer, has inspired me to solve people’s problems within my own capacity. I observed everything more intently, wherever I went and conceptualized their possible solutions on paper.

Being an artist's daughter I have always viewed the world with my own lens, and tried to capture it whenever I had a camera in my hands.





Panipuri Wagon



Panipuri is India’s beloved street snack, which is essentially small, hollow puff filled with mashed potato and spicy water. A panipuri stall can be found at every twenty meters pan-India. Selling panipuris is a very small scale business and the profit is marginal. Most commonly, a panipuri seller does the preparation at home and carries everything on his wheelbarrow and sells panipuri in a busy market, where he can attract most customers. At the end of the day, he wraps everything and carries his wheelbarrow back to house. I have redesigned the traditional panipuri wheelbarrow keeping in mind, the challenges faced by a panipuri seller everyday.

Traditional Wheelbarrow is:

  • Bulky
  • Not economic for small vendors
  • Difficult to steer in traffic
