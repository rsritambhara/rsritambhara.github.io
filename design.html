Design

As recreation, I like to design solutions for small problems that I face in my life. To enhance my approach towards problems and improve my sketching, I do this following the standard design process on a minute scale. I begin with identifying the problem, followed by researching, and then design the solution iteratively.

Panipuri Wagon

Panipuri is India’s beloved street snack, which is essentially small, hollow puff filled with mashed potato and spicy water. A panipuri stall can be found at every twenty meters pan-India. Selling panipuris is a very small scale business and the profit is marginal. Most commonly, a panipuri seller does the preparation at home and carries everything on his wheelbarrow and sells panipuri in a busy market, where he can attract most customers. At the end of the day, he wraps everything and carries his wheelbarrow back to house.

I have redesigned the traditional panipuri wheelbarrow keeping in mind, the challenges faced by a panipuri seller everyday.

Traditional Wheelbarrow is:

  • Bulky
  • Not economic for small vendors
  • Difficult to steer in traffic







Glean (Trashcan)

As a contribution to the Swachh Bharat Mission, a nation-wide cleanliness drive, I conceptualized a dustbin for public place where large amounts of garbage is generated and bins need to be emptied often.

This idea involves easy handling of garbage by everyone - public throwing waste in it and the workers responsible for emptying it. The whole process is more convenient and hygienic at every step.









ATM Redesign

As more than 25% of the Indian population is still illiterate and every mode of payment is getting digitalised, making this technological sweep accessible to everyone is challenging. I am proposing changes in the interfaces of ATMs, as I am its frequent user but still face problems accessing it, and I have also seen people struggle to navigate through it.

The current State Bank of India (biggest government bank of India) looks like:

My focus, while proposing these changes, is on the illiterate/semi-literate rural populace who are hesitant to use the ATMs and have accommodated their needs. For the urban users, I have tried to make the interaction simpler and more straight-forward than the current system. I have followed three quarters of the Double Diamond Design Process and have tried to design it ergonomically.

Also, in place of numeral entry of amount to be withdrawn or deposited, the interface can have the display of the currency denominations whose values are understood, even by the illiterates. Instructions and visual clues (such as icons) can be used to assist them in a better way. Use of Voice based feedback in case of user illiteracy can be done. Even if the user doesn’t press this, the transaction will be automatically terminated.
